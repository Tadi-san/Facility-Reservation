Build a Facility Reservation & Asset Operations Suite that runs on-site for organizations managing bookable rooms and their equipment, combining front-desk operations with maintenance accountability and quality scoring. The Angular web app provides distinct workspaces for Requesters, Front Desk Agents, Maintenance Technicians, Operations Managers, and Administrators. Requesters browse locations, room types, capacity, and availability, then place reservations that immediately reflect equipment constraints; if a room’s essential equipment (for example, projector or HVAC) is marked out of service, the room becomes non-reservable and existing holds display a clear warning with suggested alternatives. Front Desk Agents confirm arrivals and checkouts from a live schedule board, generate in-app notification banners using editable templates for confirmation and reminders (for example, arrival reminder 30 minutes before start and checkout reminder 10 minutes before end), and manage shift handoff notes with timestamps and assignees so unresolved tasks persist across shifts. Maintenance Technicians view inspection schedules, log inspection results, open repair tickets, and close them with parts/labor notes; the UI enforces a closed-loop flow so every “under repair” asset must either return to “normal” or be retired as “out of service.” Operations Managers configure promotions and discount codes for optional registration fees (free, tiered, or early-bird), publish announcements shown on the home dashboard, and review moderation queues for user-submitted notes or attachments. Administrators maintain a versioned evaluation metric library for facility scorecards (weights must sum to 100%, effective dates use MM/DD/YYYY, and historical versions remain viewable), enabling consistent audits over time.

The backend uses Spring Boot to expose RESTful APIs consumed by the Angular client over the local network, with PostgreSQL persisting all data on-premises to keep the system fully offline-runnable. Core models include locations, rooms, room types, rate/fee rules, reservations, assets, inspection records, repair tickets, task handoffs, announcements, metric templates/versions, and audit logs. Business rules include reservation conflict prevention to the minute, automatic room reservability recalculation on asset status change, inspection cadence defaults (e.g., safety inspection every 30 days for critical assets), and repair SLA tracking (initial response due within 4 business hours, overdue flagged). Optional “payments” are handled as offline order records: orders can be marked unpaid/paid/refunded, support idempotent retries using a client-generated idempotency key, and reconcile against locally imported CSV statements with checksum validation and duplicate detection; no external payment callbacks, SMS, email, or third-party logins are permitted. Security requires username/password authentication only (minimum 12 characters, complexity enforced, 5 failed attempts locks for 15 minutes), role-based authorization, and immutable operation auditing; sensitive fields such as staff contact info are masked in UI and encrypted at rest. Reliability is achieved via local HTTP caching for static assets, server-side pagination with prefetch hints, rate limiting per user (e.g., 60 requests/minute), circuit breakers around internal subsystems, a local async job queue for indexing/search and in-app notifications, nightly encrypted backups to a designated on-prem folder, and staged canary deployments monitored through local health checks and alert logs.
